narrative:
  short_form: "The share earning >$50K first crosses 50% at the Master's degree tier (55.7%) — a Bachelor's alone (41.5%) isn't enough to make >$50K the majority outcome."
  long_form: |
    Education's payoff isn't a smooth, gradual climb — it's closer to a step. The share earning
    >50K stays under 20% all the way through Some-college, crosses into the 25-26% range for the
    two Associate's-degree tiers, and then jumps to a majority specifically at the Master's tier
    (55.7%) — the first point on the curve where earning over $50K stops being the minority outcome.
    Bachelors alone (41.5%) is not enough to cross that line on its own, which is worth naming
    plainly since "a college degree" is often treated as a single threshold in casual discussion of
    this era's economy — the data says the majority-crossing point sits one credential tier higher
    than that.
